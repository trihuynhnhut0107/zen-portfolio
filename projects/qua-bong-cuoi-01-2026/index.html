Introduction

This match represented a peak tactical showdown in modern football. Both teams deployed advanced structural principles that nullified traditional central build-ups and forced play into highly contested wide channels.

Our analysis aims to deconstruct the central overloads and lateral pressing triggers utilized by the home team to isolate the opponent's double pivot.

"The spaces between the lines were extremely tight. Any micro-delay in passing execution triggered a full lateral trapping response."

The Build-up Structure

In possession, Arsenal shifted from their nominal 4-3-3 into an asymmetrical 3-2-4-1 build-up. The left-back inverted into the pivot line, while the right-back tucked in to form a back three. This allowed the attacking midfielders to locate pockets in the half-spaces.

Pressing Triggers

Bayern countered this by establishing a mid-block that transitioned into a high-press whenever the ball travelled back to the center-backs. Key triggers included:

  • Passes directed backwards to central defenders.
  • Slow body orientation of the receiver in the half-spaces.
  • Wide fullbacks isolated near the touchlines.
